Anhinga Scientific Name: Anhinga anhinga Endangerment Status: Least Concern Region: Southeastern United States, Central and South America, including the Everglades sloughs The anhinga, often called the "snakebird" due to its long neck, is a skilled underwater hunter that dives to catch fish in the slow-moving waters of freshwater sloughs. Unlike other waterbirds, it lacks waterproof feathers, requiring it to spread its wings to dry after swimming.
